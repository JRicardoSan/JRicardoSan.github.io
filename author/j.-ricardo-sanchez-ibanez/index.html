J. Ricardo Sánchez Ibáñez

PhD Student in Mobile Robotics

Space Robotics Laboratory at University of Málaga

Since very little I have felt huge passion and astonishment towards machines that ‘think’ and move by themselves, interacting with the complex and detailed environment our world is. This has become as result the cornerstone of my research career, making me dedicate my life to working with these amusing systems.

Currently I am finishing my PhD studies at the University of Malaga, in the sunny and pleasant south of Spain. These are focused on improving an important kind of algorithms that allow mobile robots go from one place to another: the path planning. I have been working on optimal solutions for autonomous ground vehicles meant to drive through unstructured terrains, which is important for the fields of planetary exploration and search & rescue. My experience includes research stays in the European Research and Technology Center (ESA-ESTEC) in Noordwijk, the Netherlands, and the German Center for Research in Artificial Intelligence (DFKI) in Bremen, Germany. Furthermore, I am also working on solutions to improve the motion planning of mobile manipulators, towards providing them with the capability to reach a site while synchronously deploying a robotic arm.

I consider the use of smart mobile robotic agents truly benefitial for our society. For this reason, my efforts are directed towards helping to make it real. For instance, these systems will be essential to reach victims of disaster scenarios and assist them, or to explore many more extraterrestrial places to help scientists unveil the secrets of the universe. Besides, I have enormous curiosity about the cognitive processes involved in the human mind and how they can inspire robotic algorithms. My interests and thoughts go hence towards learning more about these algorithms and how can they be applied on mobile robots, in order to make them somehow ‘understand’ themselves and the world and consequently become flexible and adaptive.
